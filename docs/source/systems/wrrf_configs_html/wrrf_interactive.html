WERF benchmark WRRF configurations

Process flow diagrams for all 18 water resource recovery facility (WRRF) configurations defined by the WERF agile benchmarking framework. Each diagram is derived directly from the QSDsan/EXPOsan reference implementation, not from the paper’s schematic descriptions, so the topology, recycle routing, and chemical-addition points match the simulated systems.

Source: Zhang et al. (2025), “An agile benchmarking framework for water resource recovery technologies”, npj Clean Water; implementation at github.com/QSD-Group/EXPOsan/tree/main/exposan/werf.

Reading the diagrams. Every diagram uses the same convention: liquid train across the top with the bioreactor zones in the middle, mixed-liquor recycles (MLR) above the zones, return activated sludge (RAS) below them; solids train across the middle; reject water (centrate + thickener supernatants) returned to the head of the plant along the bottom.
  • PC Primary clarifier   SC Secondary clarifier   GT Gravity thickener   MT Mechanical thickener
  • AD Anaerobic digester   AED Aerobic digester   DW Dewatering   MBR Membrane bioreactor
  • PE Primary effluent   ML Mixed liquor   SE Secondary effluent   PS Primary sludge   WAS Waste activated sludge

rBOD with primary clarifier (B series)

Conventional six-zone aerobic activated sludge with short SRT (~2 d) — only BOD removal, nitrifiers wash out. The three B variants differ only in the solids train (AD, AED, or no digestion).

rBOD without primary clarifier (C series)

Same six-zone aerobic ASP as the B series but no primary clarification, so all influent organics enter the bioreactor — higher aeration demand, less COD diverted to digestion.

Nitrifying ASP — single tank-in-series (E / F series)

Longer SRT (~6 d) retains nitrifiers for NH₄⁺ oxidation but provides no anoxic zone, so denitrification is minimal. E2 has no PC; E2-P adds primary clarification (the “prime” suffix marks a variation on the mainstream process); F1 combines PC + nitrifying ASP with anaerobic digestion.

BNR — Johannesburg step-fed Bardenpho (G series)

Step-fed four-stage Bardenpho. After PC, primary effluent is split: 80 % enters A2 directly, 20 % is step-fed to A3 along with the MLR. A1 receives only external carbon (acetate) + RAS to maintain anaerobic conditions for EBPR.

BNR + chemical P removal (H series)

Modified five-stage Bardenpho with Al³⁺ coagulant dosed to raw WW upstream of PC to precipitate orthophosphate. Carbon dosed at the anaerobic zone; MLR from the second aerobic zone back to the front anaerobic zone.

BNR — 5-stage Bardenpho without primary clarifier (I series)

Five-stage Bardenpho without PC. All influent organics enter the bioreactor and supply carbon for denitrification and EBPR — no external carbon needed. MLR returns mixed liquor from the second aerobic zone to the first anoxic zone. I3 reduces the anaerobic-zone volume to prevent nitrifier wash-out.

BNR with membrane bioreactor (N series)

Five-stage Bardenpho whose final aerobic CSTR is replaced by a completely-mixed membrane bioreactor. Solids retention by membrane removes the need for a final clarifier; mixed liquor is pumped back to an upstream zone in place of conventional RAS.
